// GENESIS — 2019

The Night We Changed Everything

It started at 3:47 AM on a Tuesday. Elliot Morison — ex-NSA cryptographer, perpetual insomniac, and the only person who ever got a perfect score on a DARPA code challenge without being enrolled in the programme — discovered a dormant rootkit buried in the firmware of 11 major financial institutions. He could have reported it quietly.

He called Harvey Pearce instead. Harvey, then a partner at Zane Specter Litt, arrived in 22 minutes wearing a Brioni suit he'd clearly slept in, carrying two coffees and a yellow legal pad. By sunrise they had the outline of Cipher & Crown Technologies.

"We realised the problem wasn't the hackers," Elliot has said in every interview since. "The problem was that the people responsible for security weren't terrified enough. We decided to make them the right kind of terrified — and then sell them the solution."

Six years later, Cipher & Crown protects over 847 clients across 38 countries, employs 314 of the most obsessively talented engineers, lawyers, and threat analysts on the planet, and maintains a running tab of over 12,000 neutralised threats that our clients never even knew existed.

// ANNALS OF THE REALM

Company History

★
// 2019

The Night Watch Begins

Elliot Morison and Harvey Pearce found Cipher & Crown after uncovering a dormant rootkit across 11 financial institutions. First office: a server closet in Silicon Landing, CA.

// 2020

The Wall Is Built

First major product — The Wall network perimeter system — deployed to 12 enterprise clients. Zero breaches in year one. Zero. Dr. Sheldon Kooper joins from CERN, calling it "a lateral move with more interesting adversaries."

// 2021

Shadowfire Awakens

Shadowfire Suite v1.0 launches to critical acclaim. Gartner names it "the most cognitively aggressive threat detection engine currently in commercial operation." We framed that certificate.

// 2022

Series B — The Iron Bank Delivers

$140M Series B funding led by Braavos Capital. Harvey Pearce negotiates terms so favourable that two investors later admit they still aren't entirely sure what happened.

// 2023

Iron Ledger & The Raven Launch

Two new products. Iron Ledger digitises legal document management for 200+ law firms. The Raven becomes the encrypted comms platform of choice for journalists, lawyers, and whistle-blowers who prefer their data to remain their own.

// 2024

Quantum Forge Opens Its Doors

Custom software division launches with 40 engineers. First project: a distributed sensor network for a national power grid. Delivery 3 weeks early. On budget. Nobody on the team slept the last fortnight, but the standups were "surprisingly efficient," reportedly.

// 2025–2026

The Game Continues

Shadowfire 4.0 sets new detection speed records. The Raven reaches 2M active users. The Wall blocks a record 3.1M intrusion attempts in Q4 alone. And somewhere in Silicon Landing, Elliot Morison is still up at 3:47 AM, just in case.
